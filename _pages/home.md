Electrochemistry brings a plethora of scientific research frontiers to aid the progress of society with pressing needs for energy resources, medical diagnosis tools, hazardous detection, and environmental remedies.

### About me
I am a materials scientists and have experience running and operating various analytical equipment that spans the spectrum of
the chemistry field. For example, using analytical aberration-corrected scanning transmission
electron microscopes (ac-STEM), to understand surface reactions at the atomic scale. I developed
the first nm thin boron-doped diamond electrode for correlative electrochemical-TEM studies
and applications. I have experience using STEM, energy electron loss spectroscopy (EELS), AFM,
electrochemical techniques and impedance, and spectroscopy (XRD, XPS, Raman, and SAXS) to
understand the electrochemical processes at the atomic scale that occur during the operation of the
energy storage and conversion devices. My research includes exploring and learning more about
the structural, and chemical changes and stability during ion (de)intercalation within the cathode/
the anode and electrocatalysis, and recycling by recovering rare and critical elements. Using
electrodeposition and inorganic synthesis, I design and fabricate new materials with desirable
functional properties that are otherwise unreachable via traditional methods; for example, the
integration of the functional electrodeposited materials (single atoms, cluster of atoms, 1D
nanobelts, 2D layers, defected nanoparticles, and semiconductor alloy materials) to various
architecture and substrate materials.
